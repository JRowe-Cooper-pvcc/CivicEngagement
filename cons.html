Cons

While government censorship may offer security benefits, it also raises concerns about freedom of expression and access to information.

Restricting access to information can become a threat to free speech by limiting public discourse, suppressing dissent, and allowing governments to silence opposition.

Authorities may also use censorship to manipulate public opinion, control political narratives, and restrict human rights in the name of “national security”.

In addition, restricting internet access can limit creativity, hinder technological advancement, and create barriers for education and world wide connection.

In other words, while censorship may have beneficial and protective aspects, it can also be used as a tool for control rather than security.
